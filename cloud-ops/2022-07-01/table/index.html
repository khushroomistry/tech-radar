Radar

A handy tool to help manage development platform and technology strategy. Radar combines the technologies used into four categories: techniques, languages and frameworks, tools and platforms. Each of these areas is ranked according to the stage of implementation:
  • ADOPT — technologies that we are confident in and recommended for widespread use in QIWI, correspond to the culture and goals of the company.
  • TRIAL — is a new stack used only in selected projects to assess risks before mass implementation.
  • ASSESS — Earley Previews, all sorts of experimental things that have potential value.
  • HOLD — is a deprecated legacy. Obsolete technologies that are not recommended for use for new projects, but it is permissible to keep them in old ones.

Goals

  • Radar preparation is an analysis of what is happening - a way to look in which direction the industry is moving, and how general trends relate to our zoo.
  • Make the right architectural decisions. Teams have a source of information about which solutions are recommended for which purposes.


Maintenance

The technical radar is maintained by the engineering community. It reflects our aspirations, successes and mistakes.
